## Procedure Snapshot
- **Indication:** Papillary or medullary thyroid carcinoma with nodal metastases, large goiter, or refractory thyrotoxicosis.¹  
- **Expected duration:** 2–4 hours.¹  
- **Special instruments:** Nerve integrity monitor, bipolar sealer or Harmonic scalpel, self-retaining retractors.¹  

---

## Step Workflow
1. **Exposure**
   - Supine, neck slightly extended with shoulder roll; 30° anti-Trendelenburg to reduce venous pressure.¹  
   - Transverse (Kocher) incision two fingerbreadths above the sternal notch in a skin crease; extend laterally if lymphadenectomy required.¹  

2. **Subplatysmal Flaps**
   - Elevate flaps to thyroid cartilage superiorly and sternal notch inferiorly; retract with Jowell or self-retaining retractor.¹  

3. **Strap Muscle Separation**
   - Divide fascia between sternohyoid and sternothyroid in midline (avascular).¹  
   - Retract laterally; divide strap muscles if exposure inadequate.¹  

4. **Medial Rotation and Middle Thyroid Vein Division**
   - Rotate lobe medially to expose and divide middle thyroid vein(s) to mobilize gland.¹  

5. **Superior Pole Dissection**
   - Identify superior thyroid artery (STA) and divide close to capsule to protect the external branch of the superior laryngeal nerve (SLN).¹  

6. **Superior Parathyroid Identification**
   - Found on posterior surface at upper two-thirds of thyroid near Tubercle of Zuckerkandl and ~1 cm above RLN/ITA crossing; preserve vascularity.¹  

7. **Inferior Pole Dissection**
   - Identify and divide inferior thyroid veins; locate inferior parathyroids near lower pole or thymic tongue, preserving blood supply from ITA.¹  

8. **Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve (RLN)**
   - Identify in **Simon’s triangle** (carotid laterally, oesophagus medially, ITA superiorly).¹  
   - Keep nerve in situ—avoid traction, cautery, or skeletonization.¹  

9. **Berry’s Ligament**
   - Divide sharply while visualizing RLN (<3 mm from ligament).¹  

10. **Opposite Side**
    - Repeat above steps to complete total thyroidectomy.¹  

11. **Central (Level VI) Dissection**
    - Remove pre- and paratracheal nodes between carotid sheaths, inferiorly to innominate vein.¹  

12. **Lateral Neck Dissection (Levels II–V)**
    - Indicated for proven nodal metastases; preserve spinal accessory, phrenic, vagus, and hypoglossal nerves.¹  

13. **Hemostasis & Closure**
    - Irrigate; perform Valsalva; close strap muscles and platysma; optional suction drain if > 50 ml/24 h anticipated.¹

## Key Pimp Questions
- **Q:** What is the most common nerve injury in thyroidectomy?  
  **A:** Recurrent laryngeal nerve (RLN) → hoarseness.¹  

- **Q:** What nerve controls pitch and is injured near the superior pole?  
  **A:** External branch of superior laryngeal nerve (SLN).¹  

- **Q:** How to prevent hypocalcemia?  
  **A:** Identify and preserve parathyroids with intact ITA branches; autotransplant if devascularized.¹  

- **Q:** Incidence of permanent hypocalcemia after total thyroidectomy?  
  **A:** ~ 1–5 %.¹  

- **Q:** Temporary hypocalcemia incidence?  
  **A:** ~ 20 %.¹  

---

## Critical Anatomy
- **Landmarks:** RLN, SLN (external branch), parathyroid glands, Berry’s ligament, carotid sheath, trachea, internal jugular vein.¹  
- **Danger zones:**  
  - **RLN** (< 3 mm from Berry’s ligament)  
  - **Thoracic duct** (left lateral neck dissections)  
  - **Inferior thyroid artery branches** (parathyroid blood supply)¹

## Informed Consent Highlights
| Risk | Approximate Incidence | Notes |
|------|----------------------:|-------|
| Hoarseness (RLN injury) | 1 % permanent, up to 28 % transient¹ | Most common cause of medicolegal claim |
| Hypocalcemia | 20 % transient, 1–5 % permanent¹ | Monitor Ca/PTH × 48 h |
| Bleeding / hematoma | 1 % airway risk¹ | May require urgent decompression |
| Chyle leak | < 1 % (left lateral neck)¹ | Due to thoracic duct injury |
| Voice change (SLN) | < 5 % subtle dysphonia¹ | Affects pitch/volume |
| Tracheomalacia | Rare (< 1 %)¹ | Longstanding goiter support loss |

---

## Post-Op Considerations
- **Monitoring:** Observe overnight for airway compromise; remove drain once < 50 ml/24 h.¹  
- **Labs:** Serum PTH at 24 h; initiate Ca/Vit D if low.¹  
- **Diet:** Normal as tolerated next morning.¹  
- **Thyroxine:** Start immediately post-op unless awaiting I-131 therapy.¹  
- **Vocal cords:** Laryngoscopy pre- and post-op if dysphonia.¹  
- **Hypocalcemia signs:** Paresthesias, tetany → prompt Ca infusion.¹  

---

## Clinical Pearls
- RLN lies **within 3 mm** of Berry’s ligament; always identify before dividing.¹  
- Superior parathyroid deep (dorsal) to RLN; inferior parathyroid ventral.¹  
- Divide STA branches close to gland to preserve SLN.¹  
- Use of Harmonic Scalpel reduces operative time without increasing complications.¹  
- Autotransplant devascularized parathyroids into SCM.¹
